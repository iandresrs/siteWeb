A wave of Algerian immigrants overtook the civil guards in Murcia, Balearic Islands and Algeria.

"Algeria completed on Thursday the migratory challenge that began in Almería on Wednesday in Murcia and the Balearic Islands. The arrival of small boats to the Mediterranean coasts (including jet skis as a new way of secretly traveling between the two countries) broke all records, with the arrival of 326 immigrants in just 48 hours, breaking all records and completely attacking the Civil Guard".

According to data accessed by EL MUNDO, on the beaches of Cabo de Gata, such as Níjar, San José and Las Negras, 23 boats circulate per day, many of which were jet skis, as official records do not distinguish by type. One vehicle with a total of 191 people on board. This extraordinary surge of boats and jet skis put the Armed Investigations Institute personnel to the test. And they quickly proved inadequate to deal with the situation.

All alerts were then triggered in Murcia and the Balearic Islands with six boats with more than 100 people on board in 24 hours at the beginning of June. The day after this alert, a semi-rigid boat arrived on the east coast. These three coastal areas are considered hotspots of illegal immigration, according to the official data collected by these new waves that have caught the attention of the Ministry of the Interior. So far this year, the Balearic Islands has received 8,702 people in 192 boats. The figure for Almeria is also overwhelming: 1,649 people and 170 boats, many of them jet skis. Finally, Murcia registered the arrival of 697 migrants in 64 smaller boats.

Jucil, the majority of the Civil Guard, will acquire a stake in the Secretary of Communication Agustín Lille after months of reproaches. “The current government and the Ministry of the Interior itself continue to fail in their work, putting an end to the illegal immigrants who arrive on our shores. Especially in states like Almería, there is an urgent shortage of Civil Guard troops. It is alarming. In Almeria, all army patrols must focus on immigrants, with the exception of civil security, before the small boats we receive arrive.” reel.

“In migration issues, the law must be applied and the rule of law must be enforced. The exercise of political command must be responsible. Jucil calls on the politicians of the current administration to take responsibility in this regard. It is unacceptable that the Civil Guard is always kept to a minimum.
