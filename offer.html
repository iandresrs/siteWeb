Vacancies to work

In several countries they need staff to go to work

The ILO (International Labor Organization) proposed a formula called "effective strategies" to help increase labor productivity, in the face of what it considers "a persistent regional lag that compromises the possibilities of finding the course of a future of work with sustainable growth and better jobs”. Each region faces "structural challenges" of great dimensions because "there are also wide productivity gaps within the countries between productive sectors, companies, groups of workers", highlighted the ILO regional director for Latin America and the Caribbean, Claudia Coenjaerts, in submitting a new report. To these, we add other external fissures that "have been enlarged with respect to more advanced countries in the framework of the acceleration of the digital transition and technological change." Coenjaerts considers that “a renewed and in-depth debate is needed in the region to address the main factors that drive increased productivity, the digital transition and the repercussions of these processes on decent work, job creation, distributional improvements and the development of sustainable companies that promote technological change”.

PRODUCTIVITY HAS DECREASED IN THE LAST 4 DECADES

The report highlights that “the majority of the countries of Latin America and the Caribbean, already before the health and economic crisis derived from the COVID-19 pandemic, showing a stagnation of both labor productivity and total factor productivity ”. “Productive labor has persistently decreased in comparative terms with respect to the rest of the world during the last 4 decades”, states the ILO consultant and author, Claudio Maggi. Maggi highlighted the importance of identifying and promoting a series of "virtuous dynamics between productivity, growth and work." During the presentation of the report, other aspects were addressed, such as the challenges caused by the digital transition and the advent of new technologies, the challenges in terms of training, the current complex situation characterized by high international uncertainty and the uncertainty before the end of the pandemic. of COVID-19, and the high informality in the region. Likewise, challenges were raised in terms of public policies, and also in the operation of companies. “The UN agency (United Nations Organizations) dedicated to the labor field in Latin America and the Caribbean highlights that the vast majority of the countries in the region already showed production problems in the face of the appearance of the COVID-19 pandemic”
