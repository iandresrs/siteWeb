The best countries to work

Switzerland

This European country ranked first on the list with the best salaries in the world. Although there is no established national minimum wage, we can highlight that their average salary is €81,609 per year, that is, €6,800 per month. The quality of life index is very high, their health is very good, which makes for a high life expectancy. It is worth clarifying that in Geneva, they had the highest minimum wage at € 3,527 Switzerland belongs to the European Union, so you could go to work with a European passport. The minimum wage per hour is €22 = FS 23.55

Iceland

One of the safest countries in the world, with an exceptional quality of life and a highly educated population, Iceland ranks 2nd for wages. With an annual average of €59,800, their average monthly salary is €4,983. Some of its services such as electricity and gasoline are accessible. Curious fact: It is a very cultured country, with an average reading of 8 books per year per citizen. 1 in 10 writes a book in his life. The minimum hourly wage (average) is USD 15.50 = ISK 2,000

Denmark

Denmark is the third in the ranking of the 11 countries with the best salaries in the world to emigrate in 2021. With an average of €58,637 euros per year, citizens earn approximately €4,886 euros per month. In addition, according to its inhabitants, it is also the country with the least corruption in the world and one of those that offers the most opportunities to undertake. As immigrants we have the possibility of applying for the Working Holiday Visa up to the age of 30, which will allow us to work legally in the country for one year; if you have European citizenship, you can also legally work in the country The minimum hourly wage in Denmark is USD 17.30= DKK 110

Norway

Norway, in fourth place, is one of the countries with the best labor remuneration in the world. Very close to Denmark, the average salary of its citizens of the Nordic country is €58,508 per year, about €4,875 per month. best salaries in the world The minimum wage per hour is USD 22 = NOK 188 In addition, it is considered by the UN the best country to live in as a result of its good public health system and its high life expectancy.

Luxembourg

The small country of Luxembourg follows close behind in fifth place, with an average of €58,040 per year, or €4,836 per month. According to surveys, its inhabitants place their country with very little corruption. The minimum hourly wage in Luxembourg is €12.50= USD 14.50

Australia

Australia is considered one of the best countries in the world to live in, ranking sixth. Its quality education, safety and health, and its strong economy in constant growth, make the salaries very good. With an annual average of €54,904, the monthly salary would be €4,575 (in your local currency AUD 7,150) One of the options to live an experience in Australia is to apply for the Corning Holiday Visa of Australia, available for people between 18 and 30 years of age from Colombia, Argentina, Chile, and Peru. The minimum hourly wage in Australia is USD 14 = AUD 19.49 Yes, we should know that with basic jobs we could usually earn the minimum or a little more, being USD 2,250 per month (€ 1,942), giving rise to one of the best salaries in the world and the most sought-after countries to emigrate. Australia offers a wide variety of jobs throughout the country, such as farm work (fruits, vegetables, harvesting), solar energy, construction, hospitality, cleaning, etc.

Germany

Germany, one of the world powers, with industries of all kinds, has a good standard of living providing jobs and opportunities in all sectors. The average annual average is €52,104, dividend per month at €4,342. It is very far from the minimum monthly salary of €1,800, but it is possible to live well and even save. The minimum hourly wage in Germany is USD 11.25 = €9.60.

The country of cheese and tulips, we place it in eighth place with an average annual average of € 54,843, which would be €4,570 per month. If we go to the monthly minimum, it remains in 2021 at € 1,700.
